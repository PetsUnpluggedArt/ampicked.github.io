            <h2>💡 Quick Tips for Teachers</h2>
            <div class="resource-box">
                <h3>Before You Choose Hosting, Consider:</h3>
                <ul style="line-height: 2;">
                    <li><strong>Student Skill Level:</strong> Beginners need simpler platforms; advanced students benefit from SSH/developer tools</li>
                    <li><strong>Expected Traffic:</strong> 20 students checking the site occasionally vs. a public-facing class blog are different needs</li>
                    <li><strong>Technical Support:</strong> You'll likely be the "IT person"—choose hosting with responsive support</li>
                    <li><strong>Renewal Pricing:</strong> Check what hosting costs year 2 and beyond (not just introductory pricing)</li>
                    <li><strong>Backup & Security:</strong> Automatic backups protect student work from loss</li>
                    <li><strong>Email Accounts:</strong> Class-based email (@yourclass.com) adds professionalism</li>
                </ul>
            </div>

            <h2>❓ FAQs for Teachers</h2>
            <div class="resource-box">
                <h3>Q: Can my students use WordPress?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Yes! All the hosting providers we recommend support WordPress. Most include one-click WordPress installation, so students don't need technical knowledge to get started.</p>
            </div>

            <div class="resource-box">
                <h3>Q: Can I get a discount for an educational institution?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Many hosts offer educational discounts. Contact their sales team and mention you're an educator. Some offer 20-40% discounts for bulk purchases.</p>
            </div>

            <div class="resource-box">
                <h3>Q: What if students need SSH or FTP access?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Look for "developer-friendly" hosts. Kinsta, SiteGround, and most shared hosting providers include SSH/FTP access so advanced students can work with code directly.</p>
            </div>

            <div class="resource-box">
                <h3>Q: How do I migrate an existing class site to new hosting?</h3>
                <p><strong>A:</strong> Most hosting providers offer free migration services. Contact their support team and they'll handle moving your site. It's usually a quick, painless process.</p>
            </div>

            <div class="resource-box">
