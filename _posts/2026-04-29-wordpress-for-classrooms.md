# WordPress for Classrooms - A Teacher's Guide

WordPress is one of the most teacher-friendly platforms for building classroom websites and student projects. Here's what you need to know to get started.

## Why WordPress for Teachers?

WordPress powers over 40% of websites on the internet. It's:

- **Easy to use** - No coding required
- **Flexible** - Works for blogs, portfolios, and full websites
- **Affordable** - Works on budget hosting plans
- **Supported** - Massive community with tutorials everywhere

## Getting Started with WordPress

### Step 1: Choose Your Hosting

Look for hosting that includes:
- One-click WordPress installation
- Good uptime (99.9% or better)
- Responsive support

### Step 2: Install WordPress

Most hosts have a simple installer. Click a few buttons and you're done.

### Step 3: Choose a Theme

Select a clean, simple theme for your classroom site. Look for:
- Mobile-friendly design
- Easy customization
- Educational templates

### Step 4: Add Your Content

Start building your class website with pages and posts.

## Tips for Teachers Using WordPress

1. **Keep it simple** - Don't overcomplicate your site
2. **Plan your structure** - Know your pages before you build
3. **Use plugins carefully** - Only install what you need
4. **Back up regularly** - Most hosts do this automatically
5. **Train students** - If they'll be posting, show them how
