# Ampicked Backlink Outreach & Guest Post Strategy

## Executive Summary

This strategy focuses on acquiring high-quality backlinks through guest posting, resource mentions, and strategic partnerships in the WordPress hosting and web technology niches. The goal is to build domain authority, improve search rankings, and establish Ampicked as a trusted resource.

**Target**: 50+ quality backlinks within 6 months

## Part 3: Guest Post Strategy

### Guest Post Topic Ideas

**High-Potential Topics:**

1. **"How Hosting Affects Your WordPress SEO Rankings (Real Case Studies)"**
   - Data-driven
   - Actionable
   - Shows expertise
   - Link to: `/wordpress-hosting-mistakes/`

2. **"Complete Hosting Migration Guide: Zero Downtime Strategy"**
   - Evergreen content
   - Highly useful
   - Link to: `/choose-hosting/` or comparison pages

3. **"WordPress Hosting Benchmarks: Performance Comparison (2026)"**
   - Fresh data
   - Unique angle
   - Link to: `/comparison/`

4. **"The Hidden Costs of Cheap Hosting (Real Numbers)"**
   - ROI-focused
   - Compelling
   - Link to: `/cheap-hosting-costs/`

5. **"Managed vs Unmanaged WordPress Hosting: Complete Analysis"**
   - Comparison-focused
   - Comprehensive
   - Link to: `/choose-hosting/`

6. **"Why Your Site is Slow (And It Might Be Your Host)"**
   - Problem-focused
   - High search intent
   - Link to: `/providers/siteground/` (fastest option)

7. **"WordPress Hosting for E-commerce: What You Really Need"**
   - Niche-specific
   - High commercial intent
   - Link to: comparison pages

8. **"Page Speed Ranking Factor: How Hosting Impacts Your SEO"**
   - SEO-focused
   - Data-backed
   - Link to: `/wordpress-hosting-mistakes/`

### Guest Post Structure Template

**Headline (Include target keyword)**
- "How [X] Affects [Y]: Complete Guide for [Audience]"
- Make it benefit-driven and specific

**Introduction (150-200 words)**
- Hook: Start with a surprising stat or question
- Problem statement
- Promise of solution
- Why the publication's audience should care

**Body Sections (500-1000 words)**
- 3-5 main sections with subheadings
- Data, examples, case studies
- Actionable advice
- Visuals/charts when relevant

**Conclusion (150 words)**
- Summarize key points
- Call to action (soft - don't be salesy)
- **Author bio with backlink** (150 words)
  - Establish credibility
  - Include link: "More hosting comparisons at ampicked.com"

**Author Bio Example:**
"[Your name] is the founder of Ampicked, a comprehensive WordPress hosting comparison platform. With 10+ years in web hosting, he's helped thousands of businesses find the right hosting provider. When he's not researching hosting performance, he writes about WordPress optimization and web performance. Check out his complete hosting comparisons and guides at ampicked.com."

## Part 5: Outreach & Follow-Up Cadence

### Week-by-Week Execution Plan

**Week 1: Research & List Building**
- Compile 50 target websites for guest posting
- Identify 30 resource pages
- Find 20 potential partnership opportunities
- Total time: 10-15 hours

**Week 2-3: Initial Outreach - Guest Posts**
- Send 10-15 guest post pitches
- Personalize each pitch (research publication style, recent content)
- Track responses
- Time per pitch: 30-45 minutes

**Week 4: Resource Page Mentions**
- Send 5-10 resource page mention emails
- Focus on high-authority sites
- Time per outreach: 20-30 minutes

**Week 5: HARO & Roundups**
- Sign up for HARO
- Respond to 3-5 relevant queries
- Pitch to 5 expert roundups
- Time: 10-15 hours

**Week 6+: Follow-ups & Relationship Building**
- Follow up on unanswered pitches (after 1-2 weeks)
- Nurture relationships with editors who respond
- Publish guest posts
- Promote published pieces

### Follow-Up Schedule

**For Guest Post Pitches:**
- Initial pitch: Day 1
- Follow-up #1: Day 10 (if no response)
- Follow-up #2: Day 17 (if no response)
- Move on after 3 attempts

**For Resource Mentions:**
- Initial outreach: Day 1
- Follow-up: Day 7
- Move on after 2 attempts

**For Partnerships:**
- Initial pitch: Day 1
- Follow-up #1: Day 14
- Follow-up #2: Day 28

## Part 6: Measuring Success

### Key Metrics to Track

1. **Outreach Metrics**
   - Pitches sent per week
   - Response rate (target: 10-20%)
   - Acceptance rate (target: 5-10% of pitches)

2. **Backlink Metrics**
   - New backlinks per month (target: 8-10)
   - Domain authority of referring sites
   - Anchor text quality
   - Traffic from referral links

3. **Traffic Metrics**
   - Organic traffic from backlink sources
   - Referral traffic to key pages
   - Traffic conversion rates by source

4. **Authority Metrics**
   - Domain authority (check monthly)
   - Ahrefs Domain Rating
   - Total backlinks in Search Console
   - Branded search volume

### Tracking Spreadsheet

Create a tracking spreadsheet with columns:
- Target Website
- Contact Email
- Article Title
- Pitch Date
- Follow-up Dates
- Status (Pitched/Accepted/Published/Rejected)
- Backlink Received (Y/N)
- Domain Authority
- Traffic Generated

## Part 9: Common Pitfall Avoidance

### DON'T DO:

❌ Send generic pitches (customize every one)
❌ Pitch to websites you haven't read
❌ Include too many backlinks (2-3 max per article)
❌ Overly promotional language (focus on reader value)
❌ Contact wrong person (find editor/content manager)
❌ Send unsolicited articles (pitch first, ask permission)
❌ Ignore editorial guidelines (read them carefully)
❌ Expect immediate responses (allow 2-3 weeks)

### DO DO:

✅ Read recent articles from the target publication
✅ Reference specific articles in your pitch
✅ Offer unique angle not covered elsewhere
✅ Provide writer samples/portfolio
✅ Follow all editorial guidelines exactly
✅ Include professional author bio
✅ Customize every single email
✅ Be patient and persistent
